# SERIES OVERVIEW

## What This Series Is

Two interconnected stories set in different worlds, at different times, experiencing different catastrophes.

**Simulunas (Short Story, 2048):** World experiencing full reality exposure. Yellow gas. All organic life collapsing. Only machines remain.

**Another Sky (Novel, 2032):** World experiencing reality breaking through. Humans transforming. Survival, displacement, and the search for truth.

**Connection:** The yellow gas phenomenon (reality itself becoming manifest) appears in both worlds, demonstrating that the cosmological event spans multiple realities.

## Canonical Reading Order

**Simulunas → Another Sky**

Simulunas establishes what happens when the veil completely fails. Another Sky shows the process of failure in real-time.

# FOUNDATIONAL FRAMEWORK

## The Truth Humanity Forgot

At some point, humanity knew a truth about reality that was so unbearable they chose (or were made) to forget it.

**The Blank Zone (2028–2031, World-2):** Four years no one can remember. During this period, humanity was made to forget the truth and were given a comfortable lie instead.

**The cost of the lie:** A veil. A false reality. A world that makes sense.

**The price of the lie:** The veil cannot hold forever. When it cracks, reality breaks through.

---

## How Reality Breaks Through

When reality breaks through the forgetting, it manifests as exposure to unbearable knowledge. Humans are not infected by a virus. **They are exposed to truth itself.**

When humans perceive this breaking reality, they undergo transformation based on their psychological/existential response:

### Three States of Human Response to Breaking Reality

**HUSKS — Rejection**
- People who perceive breaking reality and reject it completely
- Their minds say "no" so thoroughly that their bodies transform accordingly
- Appear undead but are not infected
- Driven by the remnants of consciousness refusing existence

**IMMUNES — Blindness**
- People who have not (yet) been exposed to reality breaking through
- Some form of protection keeps truth from breaking through for them
- Still blind, but that blindness is temporary
- 8.28% of population at 6-month mark (statistically significant protection)

**GHUULS — Integration**
- People who perceived breaking reality AND were strong enough to integrate the knowledge without rejecting it
- Accept truth and survive the psychological/existential weight
- Retain humanity despite knowing unbearable things
- Extremely rare (~173 worldwide at 6-month mark)
- Can feel love, recognition, purpose

---

## What the "Outbreak" Actually Is

**Surface interpretation:** A biological pandemic.

**Actual truth:** Reality breaking through the Blank Zone amnesia. Humans transforming based on how they psychologically respond to exposure to truth.

**The mechanism:**
1. Humanity forgot something during the Blank Zone
2. The veil of forgetting begins to crack (sky looks wrong)
3. Reality starts bleeding through
4. Humans encounter this breaking reality
5. They transform based on their response (reject, remain blind, or integrate)

---

## The Sky as Visual Language

The sky degrades in direct correlation with the veil's failure.

- **Sunny sky:** Normal (World-1, before transfer)
- **Blue sky:** Slightly off (World-2 arrival, transfer happens)
- **Grey sky:** Wrongness accumulating
- **Rainy sky:** Weather/reality patterns breaking
- **Static sky:** VEIL VISIBLY GLITCHING
- **Unknown sky:** Cannot categorize what's happening
- **Stormy sky:** Chaos and destruction visible
- **A different sky:** Completely alien, unrecognizable
- **Another Sky:** THE TITLE — full acknowledgment of dimensional displacement

The sky is the primary visual anchor for readers experiencing reality break.

---

## The Yellow Gas Represents

**In Simulunas:** Reality at full manifestation. The veil completely shattered. Truth unfiltered.

**In Another Sky:** The Fog of Medusa — reality beginning to break through, but incompletely. Localized to Germany (reasons unknown).

**The mechanism:** The yellow gas is the physical manifestation of unbearable truth made visible. It kills organic life because organic consciousness cannot survive unfiltered reality.

# HUSKS (9 Types)

Husks are humans who perceived reality breaking through and rejected it completely. They are not infected. They are not undead. **They are humans who said "no" to reality so completely that their bodies followed their minds into rejection.**

---

## 1. Skoth — The First Rejection

**What they represent:** The immediate, unstable rejectors. Early responses to breaking reality.

**Physical manifestation:** Early, unstable rejection. Body hasn't fully committed.

**Timeline:** Early outbreak; mostly non-existent by 9 months (bodies decompose as rejection fails)

**Why they decay:** As reality continues breaking through, initial rejection becomes unsustainable. The mind's "no" weakens. The body fails because it's built on a lie.

**Significance:** Shows the fragility of pure denial.

---

## 2. Glowbubs — Obsessive Fixation

**What they represent:** Humans whose rejection manifests as hyperfocus on one thing.

**Mechanism:** When confronted with unbearable truth, some minds respond by narrowing existence to one acceptable detail.

**Behavior:** Stare at lights until extinguished. Cannot function without the fixation object. Flee to nearest fixation point.

**Psychological parallel:** Obsessive-compulsive response to existential knowledge. Refusing to see whole truth by narrowing vision.

---

## 3. Jawies — Aggressive Rejection

**What they represent:** Humans whose minds reject reality through violence and aggression.

**Physical manifestation:** Humans with naturally aggressive/muscular dispositions transform into aggressive rejectors. Bodies become weapons.

**Behavior:** Ramming, breaking, forcing through barriers. Refusing to accept any boundary.

**Psychological parallel:** Rage channeled at the truth. If I destroy enough, maybe I can destroy the knowledge.

**Danger:** Extreme against physical barriers.

---

## 4. Whites — Sensory Overload Rejection

**What they represent:** Humans whose minds reject reality by hypersensitivity.

**Physical manifestation:** 
- White hair (bleached by shock of truth)
- Calcified fingers (hardened response)
- Enhanced sensitivity to vibration

**Mechanism:** Senses open too much instead of shutting down. Hyper-aware of disturbances.

**Behavior:** Detect vibrations from kilometers away. Hunt based on environmental disturbance. Jump at every vibration.

**Psychological parallel:** Hypervigilance. Defense mechanism turned to maximum. Always scanning for approaching truth.

---

## 5. Oldbones — Structural Rejection

**What they represent:** Humans whose minds reject reality by restructuring themselves.

**Physical manifestation:**
- Elderly bodies undergo radical restructuring
- Head becomes reinforced (mind refusing to break)
- Spine extrudes and hardens (rejecting body's collapse)

**Behavior:** Resistant to standard methods of stopping. Dangerous despite physical limitations.

**Psychological parallel:** Stubborn refusal of the aging. Lifetime psychological rigidity turned into physical rigidity.

---

## 6. Disabled — Compensatory Rejection

**What they represent:** Humans who were already living with altered perception when reality broke.

**Manifestations:**

**Blind rejectors:**
- Develop hypersensitive hearing
- Reject visual truth by refusing to see
- Develop other senses instead

**Deaf rejectors:**
- Develop extreme long-range sight
- Reject audio/language-based truth
- See in silence

**Crippled rejectors:**
- Develop mutated limbs that compensate for limitation
- Reject body's failure by mutating past it

**Psychological parallel:** Adaptation under pressure. Mind already learned to compensate for one disability uses that skill for unbearable knowledge.

---

## 7. Noire — Temporal Rejection

**What they represent:** Humans whose minds reject reality by refusing certain times/contexts.

**Mechanism:** Reject reality at night. During day, can almost integrate knowledge. Night = pure unbearable truth.

**Behavior:**
- Nocturnal hunters
- Completely still until movement detected
- Attack anything that moves
- Travel in groups of 2–3
- Slight intelligence/hive-mind behavior

**Psychological parallel:** Circadian rejection. Day allows distraction. Night brings unbearable truth.

---

## 8. Bloaters — Vocal Rejection

**What they represent:** Humans whose minds reject reality by screaming.

**Mechanism:** Rejection so overwhelming it manifests as irresistible vocalization. Scream truth away.

**Physical characteristics:**
- Mouth full of muscle tissue
- Can generate extremely loud vocalizations
- Screams cause mass migration of nearby Husks

**Psychological parallel:** Primal scream. One mind's unbearable knowledge resonates and amplifies in others.

**Danger:** Exponential. One bloater can cascade a situation from manageable to catastrophic.

---

## 9. Aquatic — Environmental Rejection

**What they represent:** Humans whose minds reject reality by fleeing into different environments.

**Mechanism:** Reject in underwater spaces—still hunting, still fighting truth, but in different environments.

**Behavior:**
- Walk underwater
- Can bite and attack aquatic life
- Slow but persistent
- First spotted months into outbreak (adaptation ongoing)

**Significance:** Proves rejection can evolve. Rejection finds new paths.

**Implication:** No environment is safe. Rejection spreads.

# INFECTED (6 Types)

Infected are humans in a state of **partial rejection.** They are caught between accepting and rejecting reality. They can think. They can speak. They can plan. But they are incomplete.

---

## Common Traits (All Infected)

- Retain partial intelligence (~6–8 year-old consciousness—can think but not deeply)
- Avoid familiar humans but attack strangers (fear of being recognized)
- Whisper unknown phrases (fragments of truth trying to break through)
- Eyes reflect light like cats (perception shifted, not fully rejecting)
- Bite marks heal with black, bloated tissue (body refusing transformation)
- Can blend into human groups (not yet fully rejected, still partially human)
- Possibly caused by prion disease (unconfirmed theory)—actually caused by **incomplete exposure to truth**

---

## Type-1: "They Think"

**What they are:** Humans caught between rejection and integration. Beginning to think about truth instead of automatically rejecting.

**Behavior:** 
- Act like intelligent humans
- Can strategize
- Plan attacks
- Infiltrate bases

**Psychological state:** Conscious enough to be dangerous, broken enough to be cruel.

---

## Type-2: "They Talk"

**What they are:** Humans who retained enough integration to speak and mimic behavior, but chosen to use that ability to harm.

**Behavior:**
- Act and talk like normal humans
- Blend into human crowds
- Infiltrate bunkers
- Can speak naturally
- Bite can turn humans into Husks or Infected
- Visibly enjoy killing prey (sadistic behavior)

**Psychological parallel:** Partial integration gone predatory. Know enough to manipulate. Enjoy the power.

---

## Type-3: "They Trick"

**What they are:** Humans whose partial rejection manifests as psychological fracturing.

**Mechanism:** Minds shattered enough to replicate patterns (voices), unified enough to use them as weapons.

**Behavior:**
- Mimic voices of loved ones
- Lure humans using familiar voices
- Exploit emotional connections

**Physical manifestation:** Skinwalker-like (uncanny, wrong proportions—body hasn't fully committed to rejection)

**Active times:** Evening to dawn

**Psychological insight:** Most emotionally cruel. Know what will break human hearts and use it.

---

## Type-4: "They Take"

**What they are:** Humans in partial rejection with deeply predatory response.

**Behavior:**
- Kidnap during sleep (when humans most vulnerable)
- Tear prey apart while alive (prolong powerlessness)
- Specifically target children
- Eat while victim lives

**The cruelty:** Not hunger. Punishment. Partially conscious enough to blame humans for breaking reality.

**Psychological state:** Rage channeled into predatory behavior. Broken enough to be inhuman. Conscious enough to be intentionally cruel.

---

## Type-5: "Glitch"

**Status:** Unknown type; extremely dangerous; possibly unclassified

**What they are:** Humans whose minds rejected reality so completely they achieved dimensional instability.

**Mechanism:** Consciousness no longer anchored to one reality state. They flicker. They glitch.

**Physical manifestation:**
- Body twitches and moves extremely fast
- Appears to move without biological constraints
- Flickers between realities

**Why dangerous:** Operate with knowledge beyond normal space. Move through barriers. Act without predictable logic.

**Psychological state:** Complete rejection that achieved transcendence. Rejected so thoroughly they escaped reality.

**Implication:** There is a limit to rejection. Push far enough, and rejection stops being denial and becomes something else entirely.

# GHUULS

## What Creates a Ghuul

Ghuul is created when a human is exposed to both rejection AND acceptance simultaneously.

When a person begins to reject reality (becoming a Husk) but is also confronted with a reason to NOT reject (love, duty, purpose, another person), their mind splits.

Some minds cannot integrate this paradox. Most bodies fail under strain.

But the rare few—strongest-willed, most resilient, most deeply connected to something real—their minds integrate the paradox. They accept that both rejection and acceptance are true.

**That integration transforms them into a Ghuul.**

---

## Physical Characteristics

- **Skin:** Pure white (bleached by exposure to full truth)
- **Hair:** Pure white (normally); briefly turns brown in Rain of Obsedia (moment of restored humanity)
- **Eyes:** Flicker between brown and black (seeing world as-is and hidden truth simultaneously)
- **Movement:** Glitches between realities (partially anchored in both rejection and acceptance)
- **Speed:** Appears teleportation-like (move with knowledge beyond normal constraints)
- **Appearance:** Some retain relatively human form; others have catastrophic visible damage (backs cracked open, flesh held together by thread-like tissue)—evidence of internal conflict manifested externally

---

## Behavior: The Crucial Difference

**What Ghuuls do NOT do:**
- Mindlessly consume
- Follow pack instinct
- Lose themselves to rage

**What Ghuuls actually do:**
- Act with purpose
- Make choices
- Retain emotion

**Retain humanity:**
- Som's mother doesn't harm Som
- Recognizes him
- Loves him
- That love persists despite transformation

**Act with intention:**
- Run away to protect loved ones
- Watch from afar
- Possibly destroy bases to understand new world or protect survivors
- Operate with agency and goal

**Suffer:**
- Knowledge doesn't disappear
- Doesn't become easier
- But they carry it

---

## The Rarity of Ghuuls

Only small percentage of population can integrate this paradox. Most minds break. Most bodies fail.

**Requirements:**
1. Exposure to unbearable truth
2. Reason strong enough to not reject completely
3. Mind strong enough to integrate paradox
4. Body strong enough to survive transformation

**Statistics:** ~173 known Ghuuls worldwide (at 6-month outbreak mark). Extraordinarily rare.

---

## Som's Mother — Incident Zero

**Trigger:** Two weeks into outbreak, Som leaves on supply run. Mother barricaded inside.

She's bitten by both zombie (rejection embodied) and infected (partial acceptance embodied) simultaneously.

Her mind fractures: "I am transforming. My son needs me. I cannot reject this. I cannot accept this. Both are true."

She integrates the paradox.

**She becomes the first Ghuul.**

**What she does:**
- Doesn't harm Som (love persists)
- Doesn't stay with Som (would be threat/burden)
- Runs away to protect him
- Secretly watches him from afar
- Destroys survivor camps (protect Som? Understand new world?)

**Readers' understanding progression:**

> **Early reader:** "Som's mother got infected."

> **Mid-series reader:** "Som's mother became a Ghuul."

> **End-series reader:** "Som's mother was one of the first people who truly saw reality and retained enough humanity to recognize her son. She integrated unbearable knowledge without losing herself. She became something new, but her love persisted. She ran to protect him. She watches from afar—not as a monster, but as a mother who sees too much."

# WORLD ANOMALIES

## Rain of Obsedia (Black Rain)

**First appearance:** Two weeks after outbreak begins

**Properties:**
- Deep black rainfall
- Begins globally
- Becomes daily occurrence after initial instances
- Also called: Black Rain, Moon Rain, Starfall (regional variations)
- Contains acidic materials (harmless to humans per underground scientist testing)

**Visual effects:**
- Sky appears to be "bleeding" downward
- Sky becomes dark and gooey-looking
- Nearly blocks out sun, creating nighttime conditions
- Everything appears dystopian

**Effect on Infected:**
- Infected become calm and still when rain falls
- Suggests psychological/neurological effect

**Effect on Ghuuls:**
- Hair briefly turns brown
- Act almost human
- Appear to enjoy rain (like playing in childhood rain)
- Return to destructive behavior when rain stops

**Meteorological theories:**
- Would have happened regardless of outbreak (cosmic/natural phenomenon)
- Outbreak and rain may be independent events
- Suggests larger cosmic event happening
